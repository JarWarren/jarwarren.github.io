Development

My first game. The name comes from my last name, Warren, which is a rabbit hole.
Built in Godot in 48 hours to simulate the pace of a game jam. All programmer art was done in Ibis Paint for iPad.
Art wasn't a focus and two days isn't much time to make good art anyways, but I did learn something from it. While a single piece of quality art is difficult to produce - even if you do, many individually good assets won't necessarily go well together. Art has to be cohesive and consistent throughout the project.
Bad art aside, I consider the biggest flaw to be the difficulty. It starts out way too hard. I've since noticed that a lot of amateur game devs do this on their first games. I guess "too hard" is a mark of inexperienced game design.
Being my first full game project, I consider it a success and a milestone.

~

Main takeaways

There's more to art than making a pretty image.
It's ok for a game to feel too easy at the beginning.
