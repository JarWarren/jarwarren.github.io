Wrrn

Development

Wrrn was my first venture into game dev. It got its name from Warren which is both my last name and a rabbit hole.
I built it in Godot in 48 hours to simulate the pace of a game jam. All "programmer art" was done on iPad in Ibis Paint. While I already knew that a single piece of quality art is difficult to produce - what hadn't occurred to me is that many individually good assets don't necessarily go well together. Part of the challenge is making your art cohesive.
Still, I consider the biggest flaw to be the difficulty. It starts out way too hard. I've since noticed that a lot of other amateur game devs do this on their first games. I guess "too hard" is a mark of inexperienced game design.
It was my first full game project though, so I consider it a success and a milestone.

~

Main takeaways

  • There is so, so much more to art than making a pretty image.
  • Don't make games too hard without a reason. It's ok to feel "too easy" at the beginning.
