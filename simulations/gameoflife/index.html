Development

Lately I've developed an interest in simulations. Relaxing games where the player provides only an initial state and watches it play out.

Conway's Game of Life is one that I've personally spent a good few hours playing over the years. The simple ruleset makes it very approachable while still being sufficiently deep to stay interesting.

For the uninitiated, the Game of Life demonstrates emergence; a phenomenon where the properties of the whole are different from the sum of its parts. Individually, cells merely toggle between on and off - but when put together, they exhibit complex, lifelike behavior.

I created the board from a single texture tiled across the screen. Live cells are just modulated blue. Each pass, a new generation is created in memory by looping over the cells and determining whether they should live or die. Once the final cell is accounted for, the new generation replaces the old, ad infinitum.

I left in the "Act of God" ability to alter the game's state while running. Technically this ruins it, but it's also more fun and I couldn't bring myself to disable it.
